Welcome to my homepage!

I am a M.A student in the Department of Statistics at the University of California, Berkeley, where I am very fortunate to be advised by Professors Aditya Guntuboyina and Alexander Strang.

My research focuses on continuous approaches to tree-structured machine learning models with the aim of improving predictive accuracy and interpretability. In parallel I study randomized dynamical systems and their connections to stochastic learning dynamics in order to understand convergence behavior and generalization in high-dimensional models

Previously, I was an undergraduate at Taras Shevchenko National University of Kyiv, where I received a B.S in Software Engineering with a specialization in Mathematical Informatics. While there, I had the great fortune of being advised by Professor Olena F. Kashpur.

I was also a Student Research Fellow in the Department of Mathematics at the Central Ukrainian State University, where I was very lucky to be advised by Professors Yurii Volkov and Nataliia Voinalovych. There, I studied the application of linear operator theory in the structural analysis of statistical models, developing operator-based characterizations for estimators in both classical parametric statistics and modern Statistical Learning.


Research interests

My research lies at the intersection of statistical modeling, high-dimensional learning theory, and stochastic dynamical systems. Some specifics are below:

  • Operator Theory in Statistical Learning: linear operators on function spaces; methods for analyzing stability and convergence; operator representations of statistical models and learning systems.
  • Randomized dynamical systems: long-time behavior of stochastic systems; convergence to equilibria in high dimensions; limits of local analysis in nonlinear systems; stability of learning dynamics.
  • Statistical Machine Learning Theory: continuous analogues of tree-based models; behavior and scaling in high-dimensional settings; Gaussian Process perspectives on learning procedures.
  • Quantitative modeling and prediction: statistical learning for financial forecasting and risk assessment; modeling credit borrower behavior and default probabilities; data-driven methods for prediction and uncertainty quantification in financial systems.
